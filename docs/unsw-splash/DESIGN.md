## Problem Statement

UNSW students at DevSoc Halftime have ~8 hours of unstructured time between programming. There's no organized social game that uses the campus as its terrain, no excuse to wander, and no demo-able "live moment" that the pitch slot can showcase as actually happening *in the room*.

User's framing: *"The app is similar to splashin however the participants are unsw students and we use their location the map will be generated with unsw buildings and ideas are to use the graphql."*

Splashin is the US college water-gun assassin game that went viral in 2024–25 (assigned targets, GPS tracking, public eliminations). UNSW Splash is Splashin reskinned with two non-obvious additions:

1. The terrain is a stylized UNSW map (hand-traced GeoJSON V1; DevSoc Freerooms-driven safe zones V2).
2. The product is its own demo: the Halftime pitch slot includes a *live finale* — surviving audience members stand at the front of the venue and proximity-tap each other under a 60-second timer while the team pitches over them.

## What Makes This Cool

The pitch IS the finale. The audience are players. The room becomes an arena for 60 seconds.

Codex independently named the same hook: *"the room watches real people get hunted."*

Every elimination during the day-long round is a public theatre moment — both phones flash blue, vibrate, and scream a water-balloon sound. Bystanders hear it across Mathews Lawn. The standing 60-second finale at the pitch is the catharsis: ~4–8 survivors stalking each other between rows of chairs while the projector shows their dots and a live splash ticker.

## Constraints

- 3–5 devs, 48–72h hackathon window (Fri evening → Sun pitch slot)
- Stack: Next.js 15 (App Router) + Supabase (auth, Postgres, Realtime) + Tailwind + shadcn/ui
- Mobile-first players, desktop projector for `/room`
- UNSW campus is the day-long playing field; Halftime venue is the finale arena
- Background GPS on web is brittle — accept foreground-only with explicit player consent
- DevSoc GraphQL deferred to V2 (API access + rate limits unverified before Friday)

## Premises

1. **P1.** Background GPS on PWA is brittle. Foreground-only tracking with the Wake Lock API (`navigator.wakeLock.request('screen')`) on `/play` keeps the screen alive while the app is open. Players are briefed at door check-in. Players who stop heartbeating for 10 min get host-pinged; 20 min of silence → auto-marked dead so they don't block their hunter. Expect ~30% attrition over an 8h day; size the player pool accordingly.
2. **P2.** In-person opt-in at the Halftime door costs **~60–90 min and 2 teammates** (9:00–10:30am Sunday). One scans QR + briefs; one handles edge cases (phone won't scan, name collision, late joiners). Late joiners (after 10:30am) are accepted but slotted into the cycle as new tail nodes.
3. **P3.** Proximity-tap with a generous threshold (~15m). False-kills are reversible via host queue. Optimize for the catharsis moment, not edge cases. **User explicitly defended this premise against Codex's challenge.** Saturday-noon 2-phone test gates whether walls/floors break it; if so, fall back to a QR-confirm second step (see D8 fallback in Open Questions).
4. **P4.** Live finale is its own mechanic — the standing 60s mini-round in the venue. **GPS is NOT used in the finale** (3m indoor accuracy doesn't exist). Each finalist wears a numbered bib with a printed QR; splashing in the finale requires hunter to **scan target's QR**. Pitch pauses; room watches.
5. **P5.** DevSoc GraphQL is V2. V1 ships with hand-traced UNSW GeoJSON + building name overlays.
6. **P6.** Privacy: a player's live dot is visible to their hunter and their prey only. Public projector renders `splashes_public` (a view that excludes `target_id`/`hunter_id` joins), shows anonymized heatmap + survivor count, no names. Names revealed at finale. Privacy is client-rendered V1, view-enforced V1.5.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Tight Build — CHOSEN

Single PWA. Hand-traced UNSW GeoJSON. Rotating-assignment-graph day-long round (A→B→C→D→A; on splash, killer inherits target). Standing 60-second venue finale. 4 pages, 5 tables, 5 RPCs. Foreground GPS only. No safe zones, no audience-gamemaster, no GraphQL, no notifications. Ships in 30–40 dev-hours.

### Approach B: Audience-as-Gamemaster + Freerooms — DEFERRED

A + DevSoc GraphQL-driven Freerooms safe zones (active classes = sanctuary) + audience-vote storm finale (audience scans QR during 60s finale, votes N/S/E/W to compress play area every 15s). Adds ~30–40 hours and integration risk on DevSoc API access. Backlog for V1.1.

### Approach C: Anonymous Hunters — DEFERRED

A but radically anonymous: photo-only (no names), unknown hunter, obituary feed on elimination, mass identity reveal at finale. Different emotional arc; same effort tier. Strong privacy story; would shift the demo from "race" theatre to "reveal" theatre. Carried as a possible direction for v2.

## Approach A — Full Spec

### Pages (4)

```
/join?event=halftime    Mobile onboard. QR-scanned at door. Captures display_name
                        (and optional selfie). Anonymous Supabase auth + insert
                        into `players`.

/play                   Main player screen. Map view, your target's name + avatar,
                        big splash button (locked/unlocking/unlocked states).
                        On mount: requests Wake Lock, refetches active assignment,
                        starts 5s heartbeat loop.
                        Realtime subscriptions (3): (1) own assignment row UPDATEs,
                        (2) target's location row UPDATEs, (3) splashes where
                        target_id = self (so you find out when you've been splashed).

/room/halftime          Projector view. Anonymized live heatmap of UNSW +
                        survivor count + splash ticker. No names. Names revealed
                        only in finale mode.

/admin                  Host queue. Initial graph assignment, undo splash, mark
                        dead manually, trigger finale. Admin-token gated.
```

### Rotating Assignment Graph

Start of game: form one cycle, P1→P2→…→Pn→P1. Each living `players` row has at most one `active` row in `assignments`.

On splash A→B (server-side, inside the `splash` RPC, all under `FOR UPDATE` locks):
1. Lock A's row, then B's row (canonical order: lower `uuid` first to avoid deadlocks on simultaneous splashes where B is also someone's target).
2. A's active assignment (A→B) marked `completed`.
3. B's status set to `dead`. B's active assignment (B→C) marked `completed`.
4. If `C == A` (paradox — only 2 alive): **skip creating a new assignment** and set `games.status = 'finale'`. A is now sole survivor; finale auto-armed.
5. Else: new assignment `(hunter=A, target=C, status=active)`.
6. Realtime fires; A's `/play` updates with C's photo + name.

**Finale trigger is manual.** The host taps `BEGIN FINALE` on `/admin` when alive count is ~6 (judgement call). The auto-trigger at 2-alive (paradox case) is the only automatic path and exists only as a degenerate-case safety.

### SECURITY DEFINER RPCs

```sql
function join_game(p_event text, p_display_name text)
returns table (player_id uuid);
-- Idempotent on auth.uid(). Anonymous auth caller. Inserts/upserts players row
-- with game_id=p_event, checked_in=false, avatar_color=deterministic-from-uuid.
-- Validates display_name (1-30 chars, trimmed, unique-per-game; appends suffix
-- like "Sam (2)" on collision). Rejects if games.status NOT IN ('lobby','live').

function heartbeat(p_lat numeric, p_lng numeric, p_accuracy numeric)
returns void;
-- Upserts locations row + sets players.last_heartbeat_at = now() for auth.uid().
-- Called every 5s from /play foreground. No rate limit V1 (trust-the-client;
-- anti-cheat is out of scope for the hackathon).

function splash() returns table (ok boolean, reason text);
-- Server derives target from caller's active assignment (no client-supplied target_id).
-- Behavior (state machine), all inside a transaction:
--   1. Look up active assignment for auth.uid() (the hunter). If none → (false, 'not_playing').
--   2. Lock hunter + target player rows FOR UPDATE in canonical uuid order.
--   3. If target.status != 'alive' → (false, 'already_dead'). (Lost the race.)
--   4. If games.status == 'finale' → (false, 'finale_use_scan'). (Finale path is splash_finale.)
--   5. Look up hunter + target locations. If either is null or stale (>30s) → (false, 'no_recent_location').
--   6. If haversine(hunter_loc, target_loc) > 15m → (false, 'out_of_range').
--   7. Insert splashes row at hunter's location.
--   8. Mark hunter's assignment completed; mark target dead; mark target's assignment completed.
--   9. If target's old target == hunter (paradox, 2-alive): set games.status='finale', skip new assignment.
--      Else: insert new assignment (hunter=self, target=target.old_target).
--  10. Return (true, 'splashed').
-- NOTE: 5s proximity hold is a CLIENT-SIDE gating animation; server only validates distance.

function splash_finale(p_bib_qr_token text) returns table (ok boolean, reason text);
-- Used only when games.status='finale'. No GPS check.
--   1. Verify caller is a finalist with active assignment.
--   2. Look up target player by bib_qr_token. If not found → (false, 'invalid_qr').
--   3. If target_id matches caller's active assignment → splash succeeds (state transitions as above).
--   4. Else → (false, 'wrong_target'). Reveals nothing about identity.

function admin_start_game(p_shuffle boolean default true) returns void;
-- Auth: admin token. Requires games.status='lobby'. Builds cycle from checked_in=true
-- players, shuffles if requested, inserts 1 'active' assignments row per hunter,
-- sets games.status='live', started_at=now(). Idempotent: noop if already 'live'.

function admin_undo_splash(p_splash_id uuid) returns void;
-- Auth: admin token. Sets splash.status='reversed', restores target alive,
-- restores hunter's previous assignment, deletes the post-splash assignment.

function admin_trigger_finale() returns void;
-- Auth: admin token. Sets games.status='finale', finale_at=now(). Marks all alive
-- as 'finalist'. Assigns sequential bib_number + random bib_qr_token to each
-- finalist (printed on numbered bib stickers handed out at finale-call). Reveals
-- all finalist names on /play and /room.

function admin_mark_dead(p_player_id uuid) returns void;
-- Auth: admin token. Used for ghost-alive players (no heartbeat 20+ min) so they
-- don't block their hunter. Marks dead, completes incoming assignment, creates
-- new assignment for the orphaned hunter toward the dead player's old target.
```

### RLS

- `players` SELECT: own row + your active assignment's target's row + the row whose active assignment targets you (your hunter) + any row with status='finalist'.
- `locations` SELECT: own row + your target's row + your hunter's row.
- `splashes` SELECT: public (projector ticker — anonymized rendering filters out names).
- `assignments` SELECT: own row (hunter or target).
- `games` SELECT: public.
- All INSERT/UPDATE: blocked at table level; only via SECURITY DEFINER RPCs.

### Splash UX (the load-bearing moment)

`/play` shows:
- **Top half:** MapLibre map with hand-traced UNSW GeoJSON. Two dots: your dot (blue) + your target's dot (red, blurred outside 50m, sharp within 15m).
- **Middle strip:** target's photo + display_name + last-seen building (computed by point-in-polygon over GeoJSON building polygons).
- **Bottom:** SPLASH button.
  - Locked: gray, label `"MOVE CLOSER — TARGET 37m"`.
  - Unlocking: yellow countdown ring as proximity holds for 5s.
  - Unlocked: pulsing blue, label `"SPLASH"`.
  - Tap → both phones simultaneously: 1s screen flash blue + 90dB water-balloon WAV + 600ms vibrate.

On the **target's** `/play`:
- Screen flashes blue, water sound plays.
- Full-screen modal: *"YOU'VE BEEN SPLASHED by {hunter_name}"* + hunter's photo.
- Below: short obituary (*"survived 4h 23m, splashed 2 targets"*) + button → `/room/halftime` (spectator).

### Finale Mode (QR-scan mechanic, NOT GPS)

**Why not GPS:** Indoor accuracy is 5–30m, not 3m. A GPS finale in the venue would either fire on nobody (threshold too tight) or fire on everybody at once (threshold too loose). Drop GPS for the finale.

**Mechanic:** Each finalist is handed a **printed bib sticker** (large number + QR code, stuck to chest/back) when host taps `BEGIN FINALE`. Bibs are pre-printed Saturday evening with random `bib_qr_token` values; the host's `/admin` shows which physical bib goes to which finalist. To splash in the finale: hunter must physically catch their target and **scan the target's bib QR** with their phone camera. Same blue flash + water sound on both phones.

Hunters know their target by name + avatar color (shown on `/play` in finale mode). Targets know they're being hunted (their `/play` shows "TARGETED" badge).

On all survivors' `/play` when finale triggers:
- Full-screen banner: *"FINALE STARTS IN 30s. Grab your bib from the host. Stand at the front."*
- Map removed; replaced with target reveal panel (target name + color + "HUNT THEM" CTA → opens camera scanner).
- 60s countdown starts when host taps `BEGIN FINALE` second time (after bibs distributed).

On `/room/halftime`:
- Map removed; replaced with **finalist roster** — names, avatar colors, bib numbers, alive/dead status.
- Big 60s timer.
- Splash events trigger explosive visual effects + survivor count tick + a `BIB #N → BIB #M` ticker.
- Final survivor crowned with a victory animation.

**Why this works in scope:** QR scan via the browser's `BarcodeDetector` API or a small lib (`@zxing/browser`, ~30KB). Already need a camera permission flow; can request it at finale time, not earlier. Reuses existing splash-event UX (flash, sound) — only the trigger changes.

### Door Onboarding (logistics, not code)

**Two teammates** at venue door 9:00am–10:30am Sunday (90 min, not 50). Roles:
- **Scanner:** hands out Discover QR cards, gives the 8-sentence briefing.
- **Edge-case operator:** phone won't scan, name collisions, late joiners, helping someone install the PWA, marking check-ins on `/admin`.

Per attendee (target ~45s each — assume queue dynamics push real avg to 60s):
1. Scan Discover QR → `/join?event=halftime` opens.
2. Enter `display_name`. Server auto-suffixes on collision (`Sam (2)`). Avatar is initials + a deterministic color.
3. Briefing: *"Your target is hidden until 10:30. Keep the app open as you walk between buildings — the screen stays on automatically. Sounds will play when someone gets splashed near you — that's expected. If you got falsely splashed, find a teammate in a teal shirt; we'll reverse it. If your phone goes dark for 20 minutes, you're auto-out — keep moving."*
4. Edge-case operator marks `checked_in = true` on `/admin`.

10:30am: host taps `/admin → "ASSIGN GRAPH & START"` (calls `admin_start_game`). Cycle randomized; all players push-notified via Realtime.

**Late joiners** (after 10:30am): accepted via the same `/join` flow. New player is spliced into the cycle as the new tail (someone's hunter retargets at next splash). One teammate is on door duty intermittently until 12:00pm for stragglers.

## Reviewer Concerns (carried forward, not blockers — engineers address inline)

Adversarial spec review (one round) accepted the doc at 7.5/10 with load-bearing fixes applied above (QR-bib finale, paradox+race in `splash` RPC, ghost-alive policy, wake lock, 2-teammate door, schedule realism, photo cut, finale-trigger reconciliation, missing RPCs added). Remaining nitpicks for engineers to address as they build:

1. **`splashes` SELECT is still publicly joinable to `players`.** P6 promises privacy via client-side render filtering, not RLS. If pre-pitch publicity matters, create `splashes_public` view excluding `target_id`/`hunter_id`. Defense-in-depth, not a critical fix.
2. **Map fade between 15–50m.** Doc says "blurred outside 50m, sharp within 15m" — leave the 15–50m interpolation as a CSS `opacity: clamp(...)` based on the meters value. Single function in the renderer.
3. **`/admin` initial graph assignment UI.** Auto-shuffle button + drag-to-reorder list. ~1h. Build after the spike.
4. **`finalists` RLS shape.** `players` SELECT allowing all rows with `status='finalist'` is correct V1 — it's the namereveal moment. Worth one comment in the migration.
5. **`splash` RPC trust-the-client (no GPS anti-cheat).** Documented in `heartbeat` RPC; reiterated here. Acceptable for hackathon; never accept for production.
6. **Real-time load test.** Supabase Realtime at 100 concurrent with ~3 subscriptions/player = ~300 channels. Untested by team. Run a Saturday-morning synthetic 30-client harness; if fan-out chokes, drop heartbeat to 10s and serve `/play` target dot via 3s polling rather than Realtime.
7. **Sunday slack.** Sunday morning has ~0 buffer time. **Hard-cut new code at Sunday 9am**; after that, only fix + rehearse + door.
