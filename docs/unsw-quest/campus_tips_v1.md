## How to use this doc

These tips and facts are designed to be surfaced as **between-clue delight** in the UNSW Quest app — short, useful or surprising facts about the campus that appear while a team is walking between checkpoints. They double as onboarding content for freshers who've never properly explored Kensington.

Format ideas (pick one per location handoff):
- "Did you know?" pop-up on the tier transition screen
- Tip card attached to a verified clue as a bonus
- Static content on the lobby / waiting screen

Fact-check all of these before shipping. Opening-hours details, equipment availability, and discount conditions change frequently.

## 1. Tech & Equipment

**Library Loan Laptops**
The Main Library (Level 1, Ask Here desk) has loan laptops you can borrow for up to 4 hours — bring your zID card. Great if your battery is dying mid-cramming session or you forgot your charger at home.

**K17 Loan Laptops**
The K-17 building (Ainsworth Building, near the Red Centre) has student-accessible loan laptops in the computing labs. Check the UNSW IT Services website for hours and availability.

**Free Printing Allocation**
Every enrolled student gets a free print credit each semester via MyPrint. Top it up at the library kiosks. Cheapest printing on campus — don't pay commercial rates at the copy shops on Anzac Parade.

**Library Chargers**
The Main Library loans phone and laptop chargers at the ground floor desk. They have most common connectors (USB-C, Lightning, USB-A). You have to leave your student card as a deposit.

**Library Innovation Studio**
Level 2 of the Main Library has a makerspace with 3D printers, laser cutters, and vinyl cutters — all free to use with booking. Most students never find it.

**Computer Labs After Hours**
The Red Centre and Electrical Engineering (EE) building computer labs are often open late during semester. Check the UNSW IT lab timetable to find an empty one when the library is full.

## 2. Food & Drinks

**Mathews Food Court — Cheapest on Campus**
Mathews Food Court (near the Mathews Building, south of the Quad) regularly has meals under $10. It's the least-known food court on campus and has the shortest lunch queues.

**Roundhouse Tuesday Schnitzel**
The Roundhouse does a cheap chicken schnitzel deal on Tuesdays. Check their socials for the current price — it's been running for years and is a campus institution.

**Upper Campus Hidden Café**
There's a small café inside the Law Library (Level 1, Law Building) that most non-law students never discover. Quieter than any of the main food courts and actually decent coffee.

**Basser Steps Vending Machines**
The Red Centre has a row of vending machines near the Basser steps that are significantly cheaper than those near the Library. If you need a snack mid-study, that's where to go.

**The Nucleus Cafe**
Inside the Wallace Wurth building (Medical Sciences precinct) — almost entirely used by med students. If you're on the south end of campus and need a coffee, this is faster than walking back to Library Walk.

**Quad Bar (Arc)**
The Quad café run by Arc is open during business hours and has student-friendly pricing. Arc members get a small discount.

## 3. Study Spaces

**Law Library — Open to Everyone**
Despite what most students assume, the UNSW Law Library is open to all enrolled students, not just law students. It's one of the quietest study environments on campus and significantly less crowded than the Main Library.

**Level 4 Main Library — Silence Only**
Level 4 is the designated silent floor of the Main Library. No phone calls, no group work — actually enforced. Best floor for exam period when the rest of the building turns into a social event.

**AGSM Study Pods**
The AGSM building (Australian School of Business, west side of campus) has study booths and pods that are underutilised by non-business students. Almost always have spare seats.

**Biological Sciences Atrium**
The atrium inside the Biological Sciences building (near the Kensington Colleges side) is a genuinely pleasant place to work — good light, not many people, and the plants help. Most students walk past it without looking in.

**24-Hour Study Rooms (Exam Period)**
During STUVAC and exam period, the Library opens designated 24-hour study rooms. Check the Library website — they're bookable in advance and genuinely 24-hour, not just "late."

**Bookable Group Rooms**
All bookable group study rooms in the Library are free via the Library website. Book the day before — they go fast from 9am. If you book and don't show up within 15 minutes, it gets released.

## 4. Campus Services

**Arc Food Pantry**
Arc@ UNSW runs a free food pantry for students experiencing financial hardship. It's in the Arc building (Blockhouse area) and operates without judgment — no lengthy forms, no questions. More students should know this exists.

**UNSW Health Service — Free for Students**
The UNSW Health Service (Goodsell Building, ground floor, near Kensington Village shops) offers GP appointments that are bulk-billed for enrolled students. No out-of-pocket cost. Also runs mental health referrals.

**Free Counselling**
UNSW Counselling & Psychological Services is free for enrolled students. Book online via the UNSW website. Waitlists can be long during mid-semester — book early if you think you might need it.

**Campus Bus (Route 891) — Free**
The 891 light rail shuttle runs between the UNSW Kensington campus and Central Station. It's free with a student card — tap on with your Opal but the fare is waived. Many students pay full price by mistake.

**Half-Price Opal Card**
Full-time students under 25 are eligible for a half-price Opal card (the "Gold Opal" concession equivalent for students). Apply through Transport for NSW with your student enrolment letter. Worth doing in Week 1.

**Lost Property**
Lost property on campus is handled through Campus Security (Basser Steps, Red Centre building). If you lose something at an event or in a building, call them first — things turn up there more than you'd expect.

## 5. Fun Facts

**The Red Centre Goes Deeper Than It Goes Up**
The Red Centre (Engineering precinct, south campus) has more floors underground than above the surface. Most of the teaching space is below ground level — which is why the building looks smaller than it is.

**Library Walk Has Over 150 Trees**
Library Walk — the main pedestrian spine between the Quad and the Mathews Theatres — has been progressively tree-planted over decades. There are over 150 trees lining or adjacent to it.

**The Einstein Bust Was a Gift from Germany**
The Albert Einstein bust on Physics Lawn was donated by the German Academic Exchange Service (DAAD) in the 1970s. It's one of only a handful of Einstein busts on Australian campuses.

**The Roundhouse Was Designed as a Drum**
The Roundhouse's circular design wasn't accidental — it was designed with acoustics for live music in mind from the start, modelled loosely on a drum. It opened in 1961 and has hosted acts you've definitely heard of.

**UNSW Has Underground Tunnels**
There's a network of underground tunnels connecting parts of the Kensington campus, originally built for utility access (pipes, cables). Maintenance staff use them. Not publicly accessible, but they exist.

**The Quad Wasn't Always Symmetrical**
The Quadrangle (between the Library and the Scientia) was extended to its current dimensions in stages — the north and south wings were completed in different decades. Look closely at the brickwork and you can spot the joins.

**The Physics Lawn Is Named After a Person**
Physics Lawn's official name includes a donor recognition that most students never notice. The "lawn" naming was informal first and later became official. Most students just call it Physics Lawn anyway.

**UNSW's First Students Were Mostly Engineers**
UNSW was founded in 1949 specifically as a technical university — the first intake was almost entirely engineering students. The arts and science faculties came later. That's why the Engineering precinct is in the geographic centre of campus.

**The Scientia Is Used for Exactly One Exam — Graduation**
The John Niland Scientia building doesn't run day-to-day classes. It's used almost exclusively for graduation ceremonies, faculty events, and the occasional large lecture. Most students who have their photo taken there have never been inside.
